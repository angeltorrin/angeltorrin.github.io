03 — How I Work

From insight to execution, without losing either.

Every project I take on follows the same starting point, regardless of the sector or the scale.

I start with the business, not the brief. Before I touch a campaign I want to understand what the organisation is actually trying to achieve commercially, where the gaps are and what the people closest to customers are seeing that the data does not always show.

Then I go to the market. Competitors, consumer behaviour, category trends, what is working elsewhere and why. I look for the gaps between what the business thinks its audience wants and what that audience actually does. Only then do I build the strategy. And when I build it, I build it to be executed, not admired. Plans that cannot be delivered are not strategies. They are wish lists.

The result is marketing that works at both ends: clear enough to get buy-in, specific enough to get results.

STORY 01

Choose Pharmacy First.

In January 2024 the NHS launched Pharmacy First, letting pharmacists treat seven common conditions like sore throat, earache and UTIs without a GP appointment. Every pharmacy in the country could offer it, which was exactly the problem. If the service was identical everywhere, patients had no reason to pick us over the shop next door.

So we built our own campaign around it, Choose Pharmacy First, designed to make Well the obvious place to go. I led the in-store and patient-facing communications across our 750 store estate: point of sale, window and counter messaging, staff materials so every team could explain the service with confidence, and versions adapted for local markets. The launch gave people a reason to think pharmacy.

Our campaign gave them a reason to think Well.

STORY 02

Two weeks to capture a competitor's patients.

When two of the UK's largest pharmacy chains began closing stores nationally, I mapped the competitive landscape against our nearest stores, analysed patient volumes and built the case for a rapid targeted campaign. In two weeks we designed, produced and deployed door drops and in-store materials. Patient registrations increased at every targeted store, one of our fastest and most commercially effective turnarounds.

CASE STUDY 02 · IVPATCH

What happens when you hand a marketer a blank page and full ownership.

Confidential project

My approach was to build the brand as one connected system, rather than treating product, packaging, marketing and eCommerce as separate workstreams.

I worked across the pieces that usually sit behind the scenes: manufacturer conversations, formulation and ingredient direction, packaging briefs, brand identity, consumer value proposition, eCommerce structure, influencer strategy and digital launch planning.

View gallery · 13

Strategy & solution

The strategy focused on creating a brand that could sit confidently in the premium wellness space, with a clear product architecture, strong visual identity and a customer journey designed for direct-to-consumer conversion.

The specific audience, pricing, product logic and launch mechanics are confidential, but the strategic work covered:

  • Audience and market positioning
  • Consumer value proposition
  • Product range structure
  • Pricing and purchase model strategy
  • Brand identity and tone of voice
  • Influencer and digital launch strategy

The aim was to make the product easy to understand, desirable, and credible in a premium space.

Execution

My role was broad and hands-on. I was not just managing marketing output; I was involved in shaping the brand itself.

I worked with manufacturers on formulation and ingredient direction, helped shape the product structure, briefed packaging designers, developed the brand identity and consumer value proposition, and built the influencer and digital launch plans.

Key deliverables included:

  • Brand guidelines
  • Packaging direction across the range
  • Product photography direction
  • eCommerce structure and customer journey
  • Launch messaging
  • Go-to-market planning

The work moved across strategy, creative direction and commercial planning, so the product, packaging, messaging and launch read as one brand.

Results

This was a pre-launch strategic and creative build, so there are no post-launch performance metrics to claim.

The outcome was a complete, launch-ready brand and go-to-market system: a premium wellness brand with a defined positioning, structured product range, visual identity, packaging direction, eCommerce thinking and launch plan.

What the project demonstrates

For me, this was the project that showed what full ownership actually feels like: not managing one channel, not executing someone else's strategy, and not working within a brand world that already existed, but helping build the product, the story, the customer journey and the commercial structure from the ground up.

The assets are redacted where needed. The capability is not.

CASE 03

CASE STUDY 03 · METATECH

The move that made me a complete marketer.

Confidential client work

After nearly a decade in retail marketing, I made a deliberate move into agency to go deeper into digital. I ran marketing strategy and campaign delivery across up to 8 client accounts at once, spanning real estate, healthcare, professional services and luxury retail, including a bespoke private members' jeweller. I owned the brief, the client relationship and the results on every account.

Objectives by sector

  • Real estate: qualified enquiries.
  • Healthcare: compliant communications that build patient trust.
  • Professional services: positioning that differentiates.
  • Luxury retail: desirability and exclusivity for a private members' jeweller.
  • And more across the book, each with its own objective and audience.

My job was to translate each objective into strategy and deliver campaigns that moved the right number for that client.

Execution

I restructured content strategy and campaign approach across accounts, then managed the creative output that delivered it: social media, content, copy, short-form video and website. I coordinated designers, developers and paid media specialists to keep everything on time and on brief, while protecting distinct brand voices and communicating with clients of very different marketing literacy.

Performance was tracked through Meta Business Suite and GA4, comparing equivalent periods before and after the restructure, with the same audience parameters and prior equivalent windows as the benchmark.

Results

Restructuring content and campaign approach drove up to a 35% increase in engagement and a 20% uplift in conversions across accounts. Alongside that, up to 8 accounts run at once, brief to results, across four sectors without dropping quality.
